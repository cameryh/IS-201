Hawaii Dream

In a distant land, far from the sea,
There lived a girl, longing to be free.
Her heart was filled with memories so grand,
Of a place called Hawaii, her dreamland.

She missed the warmth of the tropical breeze,
Caressing her skin, putting her at ease.
The scent of plumeria, fragrant and sweet,
The rhythm of ukuleles, a joyful beat.

She yearned for the waves, crashing with might,
The salty spray, a soothing delight.
To feel the sand between her toes,
As the ocean's melody gently rose.

The sunsets ablaze, painting the sky,
Colors of gold, as the day said goodbye.
She'd watch with longing, a tear in her eye,
For that breathtaking beauty she couldn't deny.

The vibrant culture, the aloha spirit,
People united, no boundaries to inhibit.
The kindness and warmth, like a tropical embrace,
She yearned to return to that special place.

But life had led her on a different path,
Away from Hawaii's shores, a moment's wrath.
Responsibilities and choices to make,
A bittersweet sacrifice, for her own sake.

Yet deep in her heart, the longing remained,
A love for Hawaii, forever ingrained.
She'd close her eyes and reminisce,
Imagining the waves, the eternal bliss.

For even though she was far away,
Hawaii's spirit within her would stay.
A part of her soul, forever entwined,
With the paradise she left behind.

So she carries the memories, tender and true,
Of sun-kissed moments and skies so blue.
With each passing day, her longing persists,
A bittersweet ache that cannot resist.

But amidst the longing, a grateful heart,
For the time spent in that cherished part.
Hawaii, her paradise, never to forget,
A treasure she keeps, her soul's vignette.
